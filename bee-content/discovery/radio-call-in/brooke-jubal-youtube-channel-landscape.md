## Key Findings

### 1. Total Ecosystem Size

- **11 channels** actively uploading
- **~1,592 videos** across all channels
- **~12.3M total views** combined
- None of these appear to be officially run by iHeartMedia or the shows themselves

### 2. Compilations Crush Individual Episodes

The data is crystal clear:

| Format | Channels | Avg Median Views | Best Total Views |
|--------|----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Long compilations (3-11 hrs)** | Second Date Update, Catch-Up Club | 37K-51K | 522K per video |
| **Individual episodes** | The Jubal Show, The Second Date, etc. | 2K-5K | 20K-85K |
| **Short clips** | Jubal Phone Taps | 359 | 1.4K |

Compilations get **10-20x more views per video** than individual episode uploads. The mega-compilation format (3-11 hours of content) pulls insane numbers — 522K views on a single compilation vs 2-5K on individual episodes.

### 3. The Audience Is There, Even on Small Channels

Even the smallest channels (Jubal Phone Tap, Second Date) pull 2-3K median views per video. That's strong for fan-run audio reuploads with no visual production. This tells you:

- People actively search for this content on YouTube
- The audio format alone drives engagement (people listen while doing other stuff)
- There's no dominant channel capturing this audience

### 4. No Channel Has "Won" Yet

Nobody has cracked 100K subscribers in this niche. The views are spread across 11+ fragmented channels all doing basically the same thing (reuploading audio). This is a **fragmented market with no clear leader** — classic opportunity signal.

## Scope Assessment

### What's the Opportunity?

**Revenue potential at current view levels:**

If ONE channel consolidated this audience (12.3M views across the ecosystem):
- At entertainment CPM ($3-$5): **$36K-$61K/year**
- At relationship/dating CPM ($15-$30): **$184K-$369K/year**

The CPM positioning difference is massive. Same content, 5-10x revenue depending on categorization.

**Realistic growth ceiling:**

Looking at comparable niches:
- OwnagePranks (animated prank calls): 5.3M subs, 1.2B views
- To Catch a Cheater (staged loyalty tests): 3.1M subs
- The ecosystem is pulling 12M+ views from low-effort audio reuploads

With proper production (animation, original content, visual elements), a channel in this space could realistically target:
- **Year 1**: 50K-100K subs, 500K views/month
- **Year 2**: 200K-500K subs, 2M views/month
- **Year 3+**: 500K-1M+ subs if format evolves (animation, original calls)

### Why Nobody Has Won Yet

1. **All channels are doing the same thing** — reuploading audio with static images
2. **No visual production** — YouTube's algorithm favors visual content
3. **Demonetization risk** — reused audio content gets flagged under YouTube's 2025 inauthentic content policy
4. **No original content** — every channel relies on radio show audio they don't own
5. **No community building** — no one's engaging viewers, running polls, creating series arcs

### What Would It Take to Win

1. **Original visual production** — animated characters, illustrated scenes, or host-on-camera reactions
2. **Create your own format** — original call-in show, not reuploads
3. **Position as dating/relationship** — $30 CPM vs $3 CPM
4. **Compilation + clips strategy** — long compilations for revenue, shorts for discovery
5. **Community features** — polls ("was she right to ghost him?"), viewer submissions, recurring characters
6. **Consistent upload schedule** — most channels upload sporadically
