## Observations

- All repurposing channels use the same source: Brooke & Jubal / The Jubal Show (iHeart/MOViN 92.5)
- Longer compilations (2-3h) outperform shorter ones on views - YouTube rewards watch time
- Top two repurposers earn $1-3K/mo combined, but recent uploads get thin views (100-1.3K)
- Niche is crowded with clones, most failing. Early movers captured the catalog advantage
- Official channels (Mojo, Morning Zoo) do better with short individual segments (8-15 min) and real audiences
- All unofficial channels are vulnerable to DMCA takedowns from iHeart
- VidIQ: "second date update compilation" = high score, low competition. "war of the roses radio show compilation" = low score overall
- Related search terms worth watching: "awkward tuesday phone call", "jubal show", "dirty work wednesday", "drama radio", "viral radio moments"

## Opportunity: AI-Dubbed Translations

As of 2026-03-22, **zero channels** are translating or dubbing radio call-in content into other languages. Searched YouTube in English, German, and Arabic — no results for translated compilations.

### Why this is viable

- **YouTube's AI auto-dubbing** launched globally mid-2025, now supports 27 languages. Creators like MrBeast/Jamie Oliver saw views triple with dubbed content. YouTube actively promotes dubbed content in recommendations.
- **Audio-only format is ideal for AI dubbing.** No lip sync needed — these are slideshows/static images over audio. The main weakness of AI dubbing (uncanny lip movements) doesn't apply.
- **Relationship drama is universal.** Dating stories, cheating reveals, awkward phone calls — the entertainment value translates across cultures without heavy localization.
- **Zero competition** in every non-English language. The entire market is open.

### Target languages (by YouTube audience size + ad RPM)

1. **Spanish** — largest non-English YouTube audience
2. **Hindi** — massive audience, lower RPM but huge volume
3. **Portuguese (BR)** — strong YouTube market, high engagement
4. **Arabic** — underserved, growing YouTube market
5. **German** — smaller audience but highest RPMs in Europe

### How to execute

- Source: top-performing English compilations (the content with 50K-100K+ views)
- Dub: ElevenLabs multilingual voices (already in bee-video pipeline) or YouTube's built-in auto-dubbing
- Publish: language-specific channels with localized titles, descriptions, and thumbnails
- Volume: start with 2-3h compilations (proven format), 3-5/week per language
- Risk: same DMCA exposure as English repurposers, plus additional layer of transformation may or may not provide fair use protection
