# Shot-by-Shot Storyboard: "He Called 911 on His Own Family — Then a Snapchat Video Surfaced"

Every second of this video has an assigned visual. Nothing is ever a black screen.

> **Visual codes** reference the [Visual Storyboard Bible](../../research/visual-storyboard-bible.md). Each code maps to a production asset with exact specs.

**Legend (Audio):**
- `NAR:` = Narrator voiceover playing over the visual
- `REAL AUDIO:` = Original audio from the footage plays
- `MUSIC:` = Background music (always playing, low volume under narration, swells during transitions)

**Visual change rule:** No single visual lasts more than 10-15 seconds. Cut, dissolve, or shift every 8-12 seconds.

## PRODUCTION RULES

1. **No visual lasts more than 12 seconds** — cut, dissolve, or shift camera within every shot
2. **Every narrator line has a relevant visual** — never black screen, never unrelated footage
3. **[TR-ZOOM] on every static image** — never show a still photo without zoom/pan motion
4. **[TR-DISSOLVE] between "mood shifts"** (warm → dark, narration → real footage) — 0.5-1s dissolve
5. **[TR-HARD] for impact moments** (verdict, key quotes, bodycam reveals)
6. **Background music never stops** — low under narration, swells during transitions, drops for impactful real audio
7. **[LOWER-THIRD] appears 2 seconds into each person's first appearance** — fade in over 0.5s, stay for 5s, fade out
8. **[COLOR-GRADE] by source type:**
   - Real footage (bodycam/trial): slightly desaturated, cool tones
   - Victim photos: warm, golden tones
   - Stock B-roll: dark, desaturated, cool blue
   - Timeline/quote cards: near-black background with accent colors
9. **Audio levels:**
   - Narrator: -14 LUFS (primary)
   - Real audio clips: -14 LUFS (match narrator — bodycam audio is low quality, needs to be intelligible; [CAPTION-ANIMATED] carries comprehension)
   - Background music: -30 LUFS (bed level, rises to -20 during transitions)
