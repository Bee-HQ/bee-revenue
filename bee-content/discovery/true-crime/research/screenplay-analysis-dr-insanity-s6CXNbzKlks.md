## Narrative Techniques Catalog

### 1. Dramatic Irony (used 4x)
The viewer knows something the characters don't:
- Officers smell the body but don't realize it
- Viewer knows Dena is lying while she speaks calmly
- Roger knows he's investigating a homicide while Dena thinks it's a welfare check
- The viewer knows the body is under the carport before police find it

### 2. Open Loops (used 7x)
Questions planted and answered later:
- What's that smell? (answered at minute 46)
- Where is Craig really? (answered at minute 46)
- Does Dena have the safe combination? (answered at minute 12 by neighbor)
- How much money is left? (answered at minute 45)
- Will Dena confess? (answered: she lawyers up)
- Why did she do it? (partially answered: money + violent history)
- Will she be convicted? (left open — trial pending)

### 3. Progressive Revelation
Each witness adds one more piece. Never info-dumps:
- Larry: "2 months missing"
- Cody: "Actually 4 months, and Dena says Mexico"
- Heidi: "No, Dena says he's at his mom's"
- Dena: "He's in Mexico with a woman"
- Ex-husband: "She said she'd kill him"
- Daughter: "She's tried to kill me before"
- Co-worker: "She told me she shot him"
- Daughter Leslie: Full confession details

### 4. Real Audio/Video Integration
The script weaves between narrator and real footage:
- 911 calls (3 separate calls)
- Bodycam footage (3 visits)
- Phone call recordings (detective calls)
- Interrogation room footage

**Ratio:** ~60% narration, ~40% real audio/video clips

### 5. Contrast & Juxtaposition
- Dena's calm demeanor vs horrifying allegations
- Beautiful mountain property vs hidden decomposing body
- "Comfortable lifestyle" vs murder
- She "doesn't keep track of time" but perfectly tracks her cover story

### 6. Strategic Ad Placement
One sponsor (Chime) placed at minute 34 — right after Roger reveals he's a homicide detective. Maximum tension = maximum retention through the ad.

### Narration Style Rules

1. **Present tense** throughout — "Officers arrive at the property" not "Officers arrived"
2. **Direct address to viewer** — "What officers don't yet realize is..."
3. **Never info-dump** — each fact is revealed by a different source
4. **Weave real audio with narration** — 60/40 split
5. **Use victims' full names** early, then first names — builds familiarity
6. **Short sentences for tension** — "She looks visibly taken aback."
7. **Transition phrases before each shift:**
   - "But that is about to change."
   - "A detail that's highly unusual."
   - "What everyone has been anticipating has finally come to fruition."

### Voiceover Notes (for AI TTS)

- Calm, measured pace — not dramatic
- Slight pauses before reveals (use `...` or `<break>` markers)
- Drop pitch slightly for ominous lines
- Speed up slightly during investigation montages
- Slow down for confrontation scenes
- ElevenLabs voice: use a deep, male narration voice with low variability
