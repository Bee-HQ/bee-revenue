> **Fair-use disclaimer**: Using brief clips of news/court footage for commentary, criticism, or education in a transformative YouTube video is generally defensible under 17 U.S.C. 107. ## 4. The Kennel Video (Paul Murdaugh's Snapchat, 8:44 PM June 7, 2021)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Description** | A 50-second video recorded by Paul Murdaugh on his phone at the Moselle property dog kennels. ## Fair Use Notes for YouTube Production

1. **Court proceedings**: Filmed in open court; generally considered public domain or highly fair-use-friendly when used with commentary.
2. **Bodycam footage**: Government-created recordings released as public records. Strongest fair-use position.
3. **911 calls**: Public records released by SLED. Same as bodycam.
4. **News clips**: Use brief portions (10-30 seconds) with your own narration, analysis, and graphics overlaid. Do not re-upload full news segments without transformation.
5. **Family photos**: Copyrighted by the families/photographers. Use briefly as part of commentary. Do not use as thumbnails without significant transformation.
6. **Getty Images**: Requires paid license for commercial use. Use screenshots from news articles instead.
7. **Court TV / Law & Crime**: Their commentary is copyrighted, but the underlying courtroom feed is generally fair use. Use the raw courtroom footage, not their anchors' commentary.
8. **Always add**: Narration, on-screen text, graphics, maps, timelines, and analysis to strengthen your fair-use position.
