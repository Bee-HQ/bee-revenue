## Content Strategy Summary

### What Works

1. **Curiosity-gap titles** — the viewer must click to learn the shocking detail
2. **"Realizes" hook** — someone discovering a crime creates the strongest emotional pull
3. **"Accidentally" modifier** — unintentional crimes drive 2-3x comment rates
4. **Clean, direct titles** — no emoji, no brackets, no clickbait formatting
5. **Medium length titles** (31-50 chars) outperform longer ones
6. **Long-form content** (~39 min) — maximizes ad revenue per video and watch time
7. **Low frequency, high quality** — ~2.5 videos/month, each pulling 10M+ views
8. **Thursday uploads** perform best; avoid Saturday

### What Could Be Tested

1. **More question titles** — only 3 used, but they outperform statements by 28%
2. **"How Police Captured" series** — the 2 videos using this format averaged 16.6M views
3. **"Accidentally" themed content** — highest engagement scores but only 3 videos made
4. **Shorter titles** — only 3 videos under 30 chars, but they performed comparably
5. **Shifting uploads to Thursday** — currently most uploads are Friday (worst performing weekday)

### Replicability Assessment

| Factor | Difficulty | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Title formula | Easy | Highly templated, can be replicated |
| Content research | Moderate | Requires finding compelling true crime cases |
| Narration quality | Moderate | Needs professional-quality AI voice or narrator |
| Visual production | Moderate | Crime footage, courtroom clips, reenactments |
| Audience building | Hard | True crime niche is competitive; 4.3M subs took years |
| Consistency | Hard | Maintaining 10M+ views per video requires exceptional taste in case selection |
